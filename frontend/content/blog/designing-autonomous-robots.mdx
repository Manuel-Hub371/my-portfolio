Autonomous mobile robots require tight integration between perception, localization, planning, and control. Here's how I approach system design.

## The Perception Stack

Cameras and LiDAR provide complementary data. For indoor AMRs, 2D LiDAR plus depth cameras often suffice. Fuse inputs with a consistent timestamp using `message_filters` in ROS 2.

## Localization & Mapping

Start with **SLAM Toolbox** or **Cartographer** for mapping, then switch to **AMCL** or **localization-only** modes for deployment.

## Navigation with Nav2

Nav2's behavior trees let you compose recovery behaviors:

- Rotate in place when stuck
- Back up and replan on failure
- Dynamic obstacle handling with costmap updates

## Testing Strategy

Simulate in Gazebo before hardware tests. Record rosbags from production runs to replay edge cases offline.

## Results

Proper tuning reduced navigation failures by 40% in cluttered warehouse aisles — mostly from costmap inflation and controller gain adjustments.
